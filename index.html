The story of terrabarn.

Terra Barn a cottage industry, in the year 2002 was established to revive the traditional art of Terracotta into contemporary jewellery form that hand manufactures ethnic terracotta jewellery, which supplies to local, national and international clients.

Origin

Poornima

Terra Barn was founded by Poornima S Prasad , a social entrepreneur, Trainer and a certified Artisan from RDTDC, (Regional Design and Technical Development Centre, Govt. of India).

Terra Barn provides income generation opportunities to marginalised women by training them in the art of terracotta jewellery making and support  in marketing which works towards sustainable livelihood. Several women who work at Terra Barn have learnt this art and they are now economically independent. They  work close to their homes and able to financially support their families.

Connections

Terra Barn was a part of Launch Pad 8 of NSRCEL, an IIMB initiative. Terra Barn is listed as official artisan with CCIC – Central Cottage Industries, CCI – Craft Council of Tamil Nadu and GoCoop Solutions & Services Pvt Ltd. Terra Barn also partners with various brands as customer, tie-up with handicrafts / sales outlets like, Kodava Handicrafts at Coorg and Individuals who participate at exhibitions.

About the founder

An artist herself, Poornima loves to create beautiful rustic pieces from clay and is passionate about reaching out to more artists and popularising this art. With over 15 years of experience in the field, Poornima is a master crafts person and a trainer in terracotta jewellery making and has worked tirelessly to provide vocational training and livelihood opportunities to economically marginalized women.
